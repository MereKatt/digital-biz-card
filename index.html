Greetings from Oklahoma City!

I started my journey in graphic design as a teenager working at a small-town newspaper. After completing a BA in journalism in 2005, my career has primarily focused on working with nonprofit organizations. Today, my passion for life-long learing has brought me to a new chapter - learning to code!

About my work

I brand myself as a "utility player" and love to work in fast-paced environments. I am drawn to coding because I enjoy collaborating with a team to manage projects from conception to completion. My two most common catchphrases when I'm a project lead are:

  1. "Work hard, play hard."
  2. "Team work makes the dream work!"

A few of my career highlights include:

  • Winning an Our Town Grant from the National Endowment for the Arts to create a Public Art Masterplan for the City of Oklahoma City
  • Leading a capital fundraising campaign for Oklahoma Contemporary Arts Center to build a state-of-the-art campus in downtown OKC
  • Managing a temporary public art exhibition to commemorate the 50th Anniversary of the Allied Arts Foundation, including a public block party attended by more than 10,000 guests
  • Starting my own business as a freelance designer and event producer following the COVID-19 pandemic

About me

As you might guess, art plays a big role in my life. I have been an avid collector for more than a decade, and enjoy several creative practices including hand lettering, painting and embroidery.

I also have two pets which are the light of my life: Frank the cat, and Nym the German Shepherd.
